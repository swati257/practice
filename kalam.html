Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

About

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

Early Life

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam came from a humble background. Despite financial challenges, he pursued his education with determination and graduated in aerospace engineering.

Achievements

  • Key contributor to India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Developed ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.
  • Authored several inspirational books, including Wings of Fire.
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam inspired millions with his vision for a developed India by 2020. He was deeply loved for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to education and youth empowerment.
