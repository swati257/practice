Lata Mangeshkar

Born: September 28, 1929

Died: February 6, 2022

Lata Mangeshkar, often referred to as the "Nightingale of India," was one of the most celebrated playback singers in the history of Indian cinema. With a career spanning over seven decades, she recorded songs in over 36 Indian languages and a few foreign languages.

Her melodious voice and unmatched versatility earned her numerous accolades, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 2001. She was also honored with the Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan, and several National Film Awards.

Some of her most iconic songs include "Lag Jaa Gale," "Ajeeb Dastan Hai Yeh," and "Tere Bina Zindagi Se Koi."
