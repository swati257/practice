Banyan Tree

Overview

The Banyan tree (Ficus benghalensis) is a large and iconic tree native to the Indian subcontinent. Known for its sprawling canopy and aerial roots, it is often considered a symbol of immortality and strength in Indian culture.

Key Features

  • Scientific Name: Ficus benghalensis
  • Family: Moraceae
  • Height: Can grow up to 30 meters tall
  • Special Feature: Aerial roots that grow into thick trunks

Cultural Significance

The Banyan tree holds a sacred place in many cultures, especially in India, where it is associated with longevity and wisdom. It is often found near temples and is a common subject in folklore and mythology.
